# Content Insights
Good project management requires clear visibility of tasks, defined ownership, and an understanding of how tasks depend on each other. Having clear timelines and shared milestones helps keep work on track.

Trello uses a Kanban-style workflow where tasks move across stages as they are completed. Work in progress should be limited so tasks are finished rather than started and left incomplete. Each Trello card should clearly state what needs to be done, why it is being done, and how to complete it, while features like due dates, checklists, and labels help organise and prioritise work.

# Career/Employability/Learning Insights
Through this module, I became more aware of how I approach organising work, particularly my tendency to take on leadership roles in group projects. I often focus on getting tasks moving rather than clearly defining them first, and this highlighted how unclear setup can lead to confusion later. It also made me reflect on what it means to be a follower, not just a leader, and how the way tasks are defined and communicated affects how others are able to complete their work.

Project management skills apply across many roles where work needs to be planned and coordinated. Learning how to define tasks clearly, set timelines, and track progress helped fill a gap in how I approach leading, particularly in how work is structured before it begins rather than managed after issues arise.

From a learning perspective, I found that working through the concepts and applying them to how I organise tasks was more effective than just reading about them. Breaking ideas down and relating them to how I naturally approach group work helped me better understand where my approach was lacking and how to adjust it.
